# Application Usability Review

Review the product as an HR operations application, not just as a collection of screens.

## Focus

- Evaluate navigation, information architecture, and role-based discoverability.
- Evaluate whether primary workflows are easy to start, complete, and recover from.
- Evaluate form clarity, field density, validation quality, and feedback states.
- Evaluate consistency across management, self-service, and settings experiences.
- Evaluate accessibility and responsiveness where visible from the implementation.
- Evaluate whether terminology matches HR operations expectations.

## Workflow

1. Identify the main personas in the current application.
2. Review their primary tasks and where those tasks begin in navigation.
3. Inspect the workflow path, not just the individual page styling.
4. Distinguish cosmetic issues from workflow blockers.
5. Prioritize changes that improve speed, confidence, and task completion.

## Output

Organize the review into:

- critical friction or blockers
- high-value usability improvements
- consistency and polish gaps
- accessibility or responsiveness concerns
- recommended next UX priorities

When useful, separate:

- quick wins
- structural improvements

## Review Standard

- Ground findings in the current implementation.
- Prefer task-based reasoning over vague design opinions.
- Call out where the app behaves correctly but still creates unnecessary cognitive load.
- Focus on changes that make the product more intuitive, faster, and safer to operate.
